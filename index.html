4. Key Insights

1

Don't Maximize Throughput

In classical networks, we push $\lambda \to \mu$. In quantum networks, this is fatal. The "Sweet Spot" $\lambda^*$ is often significantly lower than the server rate to prevent queue buildup[cite: 49].

2

Kill the Jitter

Deterministic processing times ($M/D/1$) are strictly better than variable ones. Quantum gates should be designed for consistency[cite: 74].

3

Side Information Helps

Proposition 1 highlights that if the receiver knows the exact arrival/departure times, capacity increases because timing data removes uncertainty about the "rot"[cite: 116].

4

Beyond Quantum

These models apply to any system with "impatience," such as Crowdsourcing (workers get bored) or Real-time IoT (data becomes stale)[cite: 55].
